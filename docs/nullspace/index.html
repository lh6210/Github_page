NOTES:
  • The nullspace $N(A) \in \mathcal{R}^n$ contains all solutions $\mathbf{x}$ to $A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{0}$. This includes $\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
  • What is a free column and a pivot column of matrix $A$?
    A pivot column of matrix $A$ is a column of $A$ that has the pivot. A free column of $A$ is a column of $A$ that has no pivot. Whether a column is pivot or free is easier to see in $A$'s row reduced echelon form $R$.
    Every free column is a combination of some pivot columns. It is the special solutions that tell us those combinations.
  • What is a special solution to $A \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$?
    A special solution belongs to $N(A)$ and it corresponds to a specific free column of matrix $A$. Suppose $J = \{j \in \mathcal{Z} \mid j \text{th column of A is a free column} \} $. If column $i$ of $A$ is free, then we define the special solution corresponding to it as having $\mathbf{x}_i = 1$ and $\mathbf{x}_j = 0, \forall j \in J \; \text{and} \; j \neq i $.
  • What is a free variable in solution $\mathbf{x}$ to $A \mathbf{x} = 0$?
    Suppose $J = \{j \in \mathcal{Z} \mid j \text{th column of A is a free column} \} $. A free variable of $\mathbf{x} \in \mathcal{R}^n \;$ is any $\mathbf{x}_j \; \text{such that} \; j \in J$. In another word, the position $j$ of $\mathbf{x}_j$ depends on the position of free columns of matrix $A$. The number of free variables equals to the number of free columns in matrix $A$, or the number of columns of $A$ minus the number of pivots in $A$.
  • The nullspace of $A$ consists of all combinations of the special solutions to $A \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
  • The nullspaces of matrix $A$, $A$'s upper triangular $U$, and $A$'s row reduced echelon form $R \;$ stay the same: $N(A) = N(U) =N(R)$. This nullspace becomes easier to see when we reach the reduced row echelon form $R = rref(A)$. The pivot columns of $R$ contain $I$.
  • An invertible matrix $A$ has no free columns or special solutions in $N(A)$, because all of its columns have pivots. Given an invertible matrix $A$, the solution to $A \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$ is trivial, just $\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$.
  • If $N(A)$ is trivial, just $\mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$, then the columns of $A$ are independent.
  • Suppose $A \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$ has more unknowns than equations ($n \gt m $, more columns than rows ). There must be at least one free column. Then $A \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$ has nonzero solutions.
  • A short wide matrix ( $n \gt m$ ) always has nonzero vectors in its nullspace. There must be at least $n - m$ free variables, since the number of pivots cannot exceed $m$.
  • The nullspace is a subspace. Its dimension is the number of free variables. When there is a free varialbe, it can be set to 1. Then the equation $A \mathbf{x} = \mathbf{0}$ has at least a line of nonzero solutions.
  • The rank of $A$ is the number of pivots. This number is $r$. So then the rank of $N(A)$ is $n - r$.
